# Michigan Residential Code 2021 (IRC 2021) — Quick Reference

> **Source:** [UpCodes — Michigan Residential Code 2021](https://up.codes/viewer/michigan/mi-residential-code-2021)
> (merged text: International Residential Code 2021 + Michigan amendments). Body text read openly from
> UpCodes (ICC content is ungated).
>
> ## 🏠 THIS is the code for your house
> The MRC governs **detached one- & two-family dwellings and townhouses ≤ 3 stories** — i.e., this
> project. It is *self-contained*: building, energy, mechanical, fuel-gas, plumbing, and electrical all
> in one book. Where the MRC is silent, the parent codes (MBC/IPC/NEC) fill in. Companion files:
> `Michigan-Building-Code-2021-reference.md` (larger structures / engineering backbone),
> `Michigan-Plumbing-Code-2021-reference.md`, `Michigan-Electrical-Code-2023-reference.md`.
>
> Units in US customary as the code uses them. `o.c.` = on center, `psf` = lb/ft², `f′c` = concrete
> compressive strength. Section IDs are the IRC "R" numbers (e.g., **R305.1**).

# PART I–II — Administration & Definitions (Ch 1–2)
- **R101–R114** — scope (1- & 2-family dwellings & townhouses ≤ 3 stories), permits, inspections, CO. Michigan administers via the R 408 rule series.
- **Ch 2** — defined terms (habitable space, grade plane, story, braced wall line, etc.) that drive thresholds.

# CHAPTER 3 — Building Planning 🏠 (the dwelling-design chapter — know this one)

### R301 Design criteria / loads
- Live loads: **floors 40 psf**, **sleeping rooms 30 psf**, decks/balconies **40 psf**, stairs 40 psf, uninhabitable attic w/ limited storage 20 psf (10 psf w/o).
- Snow/wind/seismic per **Table R301.2** + **Michigan maps** — put county values on drawings. MI: ground snow **~20–60+ psf** by county; wind **~115 mph** Vult; mostly **Seismic Design Cat A/B**.

### R302 Fire-resistant construction 🏠
- **Garage–dwelling separation (R302.6):** **½-in gypsum** on the garage side of common walls; **⅝-in Type X** on the garage ceiling where habitable rooms are above; structure supporting that ceiling also protected.
- **Garage opening protection (R302.5.1):** door into the house = **solid wood/steel ≥ 1⅜ in or 20-minute rated, self-closing & self-latching**; **no door directly into a sleeping room**.
- **R302.11 Fireblocking** (10 ft o.c. horizontal, at ceiling/floor, soffits, stair stringers); **R302.12 Draftstopping** (floor-ceiling assemblies > 1,000 ft²).
- **Townhouse separation (R302.2):** 1-hr (or 2× 1-hr) wall, or common 2-hr; openings/penetrations limited.

### R303 Light, ventilation, heating 🏠
- Natural light ≥ **8%** of floor area; ventilation openable ≥ **4%** (or whole-house mechanical per M1505).
- Bathrooms: openable window **or** mechanical exhaust **≥ 50 cfm** (intermittent) / 20 cfm (continuous).
- Habitable rooms must be heatable to **≥ 68 °F** at 3 ft AFF.

### R304 Minimum room area — habitable room ≥ **70 ft²**, ≥ **7 ft** in any horizontal dimension.

### R305 Ceiling height — **7 ft** habitable/hallways; **6 ft 8 in** bathrooms, laundry, basements (beams may drop to 6 ft 4 in). Sloped ceilings: ≥ 50% of required area at full height.

### R306 Sanitation — each dwelling needs a water closet, lavatory, tub/shower, kitchen sink; connect to approved water & sewer (→ Ch 25–33 / Plumbing Code).

### R307 Toilet/bath clearances 🏠 — water closet **15 in** centerline-to-wall (so **30 in** total width), **21 in** clearance in front; showers ≥ **900 in²** & ≥ 30 in (per P2708).

### R308 Glazing & safety glazing 🏠 — hazardous locations require safety (tempered/laminated) glazing:
- In **doors** & storm doors; **within 24 in** of a door edge where bottom < 60 in AFF;
- **Windows** > 9 ft² with bottom < 18 in AFF and top > 36 in AFF and walking surface within 36 in;
- **Tub/shower/sauna/pool** walls where glazing bottom < **60 in** above the standing surface;
- Adjacent to **stairs/landings/ramps** within 36 in and < 60 in above the walking surface.

### R309 Garages & carports — floor sloped to drain (≥ 2%); separation per R302; no Class I/II liquid floor drains to sewer.

### R310 Emergency escape & rescue openings (EERO) 🏠 — required in **every sleeping room** and **basements**: net clear **5.7 ft²** (**5.0 ft²** at grade floor), min height **24 in**, min width **20 in**, **sill ≤ 44 in** AFF; window wells ≥ 9 ft² with ladder if > 44 in deep.

### R311 Means of egress 🏠 — at least one **side-hinged egress door 32 in clear × 78 in**; landing each side (≤ 1.5 in below threshold); hallways **≥ 36 in**. **Stairs:** width ≥ **36 in** above handrail, riser ≤ **7¾ in**, tread ≥ **10 in**, headroom ≥ **6 ft 8 in**, nosing ¾–1¼ in; **handrail 34–38 in** continuous; landings ≥ 36 in. Ramps max slope **1:12** (or 1:8 where 1:12 impractical).

### R312 Guards & window fall protection 🏠 — **guards ≥ 36 in** where walking surface > **30 in** above grade/floor; openings reject a **4-in sphere** (stair side **4⅜ in**, triangle at treads 6 in). **Window fall protection (R312.2):** operable window sill **< 24 in** AFF and **> 72 in** above exterior grade → limiter or guard.

### R313 Automatic sprinklers — **NFPA 13D** required for new townhouses (and 1-/2-family where adopted; verify MI amendment — MI has historically NOT mandated 13D for 1- & 2-family dwellings).

### R314 Smoke alarms 🏠 — in **each sleeping room**, **outside** each sleeping area, on **each story** (incl. basement); **interconnected**, hard-wired with battery backup (battery-only allowed in some alterations).

### R315 CO alarms 🏠 — **outside each sleeping area** where a **fuel-fired appliance** exists or an **attached garage**.

### R316 Foam plastic — thermal barrier (**½-in gypsum or 15-min equivalent**) over foam in interior; ignition-barrier in attics/crawls; flame spread ≤ 75 / smoke ≤ 450.

### R317–R318 Decay & termite 🏠 — pressure-treated/naturally-durable wood: sills on concrete/masonry, wood **< 6 in** to exposed earth, joists < 18 in / girders < 12 in over crawl ground, deck framing; MI termite provisions per local designation.

### R319–R330 — address numbers; flood-resistant (R322); solar (R324); **mezzanines (R325, ≤ 1/3 area, 7 ft clear)**; habitable attics (R326); **pools/spas/hot tubs (R327 → barrier ≥ 48 in, gate self-closing/latching, 4-in sphere, anti-entrapment drains)**; energy-storage systems (R328), generators (R329), fuel cells (R330).

# CHAPTER 4 — Foundations 🏠
- **R403 Footings** — min **12 in wide × 6 in deep**; **≥ 42 in below grade** (MI frost) or frost-protected shallow foundation (R403.3); projection 2 in–footing thickness; width by soil bearing (Tables R403.1).
- **Concrete (Table R402.2)** — f′c **2,500–3,500 psi**; **MI = Severe/Moderate weathering → air-entrained 5–7%** for exterior/garage slabs & exposed concrete (garage slab **3,500 psi**).
- **Anchor bolts (R403.1.6)** — **≥ ½-in dia**, **≤ 6 ft o.c.**, **≥ 7 in** embed, ≥ 2 per plate, one within 12 in of each end.
- **R404 Foundation walls** — concrete/masonry thickness & reinforcement by height/soil/backfill (Tables R404.1.1) — 6–16 in range.
- **R405 Drainage** — perforated drain tile in gravel around footings in wet/poorly-draining soils, daylight or sump.
- **R406 Damp/waterproofing** — dampproof below-grade walls enclosing habitable/usable space; **waterproof** where a high water table/severe soil.
- **R408 Crawl space** 🏠 — vent **1 ft² per 150 ft²** (1/1500 with Class I vapor retarder + mechanical), unless conditioned; access **18 × 24 in**; **6-mil poly** ground cover.
- **R506 Slab-on-grade** — ≥ **3½ in** concrete, 4-in gravel base, **≥ 10-mil** vapor retarder (6-in laps) under habitable slabs.

# CHAPTER 5 — Floors 🏠
- **R502 Wood floor framing** — joist spans by species/grade/spacing (**12 / 16 / 19.2 / 24 in o.c.**), Tables R502.3.1; e.g., DF-L #2 2×10 @ 16 in ≈ **14′2″** (40 psf LL).
- **Notch/bore (R502.8)** 🏠 — notch ≤ **1/6 depth**, ≤ 1/3 long, **not in middle 1/3 of span**; holes ≤ **1/3 depth**, ≥ **2 in** from edges/other holes. (Engineered I-joists: per manufacturer only.)
- **Bearing (R502.6)** — **1½ in** on wood/metal, **3 in** on masonry; lapped joists 3 in + three 10d.
- **R503 Sheathing** — min subfloor thickness by span (e.g., **19/32–23/32 in** WSP at 16–24 in o.c.).
- **R502.3.3 Cantilevers** ≤ joist depth (tables for wall/roof vs balcony); **R507 Decks** — treated wood, ledger bolting (R507.9), guard/load per R301.

# CHAPTER 6 — Wall Construction 🏠
- **R602.3 Studs** — **2×4 @ 16 in o.c.** to **10 ft** (bearing, roof+ceiling); **2×6 @ 24 in o.c.** to 10 ft (roof + one floor); nonbearing 2×4 @ 24 in to 14 ft (Table R602.3(5)).
- **Plates** — **double top plate** (laps ≥ 24 in offset) on bearing walls; single allowed with tie plate; bottom plate ≥ stud width.
- **Notch/bore (R602.6)** 🏠 — **notch** ≤ **25%** stud depth (bearing) / **40%** (nonbearing); **bore** ≤ **40%** (≤ 60% if doubled & not adjacent), ≥ **⅝ in** from edge, not in same section as a notch.
- **Headers (Table R602.7)** — span by size/load/building width (e.g., 2-ply 2×10 ≈ 5–6 ft at 12 ft width, 30 psf snow).
- **Fireblocking R602.8** (per R302.11): **10 ft** horizontal, at floor/ceiling, soffits, around penetrations.
- **Wall bracing R602.10** 🏠 — braced-wall lines ≤ **60 ft** apart (wind < 140 mph, Exp B); panels begin within **10 ft** of each end, ≤ **20 ft** between panels; method **WSP/CS-WSP** etc. with min lengths (Table R602.10.3).
- **Fastening (Table R602.3(1))** — stud-to-plate 4×8d toenail / 2×16d end-nail; **sheathing 8d @ 6 in edges / 12 in field**.

# CHAPTER 7 — Wall Covering 🏠
- **R702 Interior** — gypsum **½ or ⅝ in**, fasteners 7 in (ceilings) / 8 in (walls) o.c. nailed; **tub/shower walls** need **cement/glass-mat/fiber-cement backer** (regular water-resistant board not in direct-water areas); not behind tile in wet areas if paper-faced.
- **R703 Exterior** — **≥ 1 layer water-resistive barrier** (No. 15 felt / ASTM D226 Type I; laps 2 in horiz, 6 in vert); **flashing** shingle-fashion at all openings/penetrations/roof intersections/kickout.
- **Siding (Table R703.3(1))** — min thickness: wood **⅜ in**, fiber-cement **5/16 in**, vinyl **0.035 in**; nail to studs.
- **Anchored masonry veneer (R703.8)** — **1-in air gap**, ties **1 per 2.67 ft²** (≤ 24×32 in), **weep holes ≤ 33 in o.c.**, flashing + weeps at base.
- **Clearances** 🏠 — siding **6 in** above grade (2 in above paving), **2 in** above roof surfaces.

# CHAPTER 8 — Roof-Ceiling Construction 🏠
- **R802 Rafters/ceiling joists** — spans by species/grade/spacing/snow/dead (Tables R802.4 rafters, R802.5 ceiling joists). MI uses the **snow-load** columns (20/30/50/70 psf).
- **Ridge board** ≥ **1 in nominal** and ≥ depth of rafter cut; **ridge beam** required where no rafter ties.
- **Rafter/collar ties** 🏠 — **rafter ties** (often the ceiling joists) at the bottom resist thrust; **collar ties ≥ 1×4 @ ≤ 4 ft o.c.** in the **upper third**.
- **Notch/bore (R802.7)** — same 1/6, 1/3, 2-in rules as joists; no notches in middle third; no holes/notches in the outer third of cantilever except per rule.
- **R803 Sheathing** — min thickness by rafter spacing & load (Table R803.1).
- **R806 Attic ventilation** 🏠 — net free area **1/150** of attic (or **1/300** with balanced ridge+soffit vents or a Class I vapor retarder); vents ≥ 1 in airspace above insulation (baffles). Unvented attic option per R806.5.
- **R807 Attic access** — **22 × 30 in** where attic > 30 in clear height, 30 in headroom.

# CHAPTER 9 — Roof Assemblies 🏠
- **R905 Min slopes**: asphalt shingles **2:12** (double underlayment 2:12–4:12); clay/concrete tile **2½:12**; metal panels ¼:12 (standing-seam) / 3:12 (lapped no sealant); metal shingles **3:12**; wood shingles **3:12**; wood shakes **3:12**; mineral roll **1:12**.
- **Underlayment** — one layer ≥ 4:12; **two layers** 2:12–4:12.
- **R905.1.2 Ice barrier** 🏠 (required across MI) — self-adhering membrane from **eave edge to ≥ 24 in inside the exterior wall line**.
- **Fastening** — asphalt shingles **≥ 4 nails** per strip (6 in high-wind); **drip edge** at eaves & rakes, extends ≥ ¼ in below deck and ≥ 2 in onto deck, fastened ≤ 12 in o.c.

# CHAPTER 10 — Chimneys & Fireplaces 🏠
- **R1001 Masonry fireplace** — **hearth extension**: opening < 6 ft² → **16 in front / 8 in each side**; ≥ 6 ft² → **20 in / 12 in**. Firebox depth ≥ **20 in**; walls **10 in solid masonry** (or **8 in with firebrick lining**). Combustible clearance **2 in** (front/side), **4 in** back; mantel/trim ≥ 6 in from opening (+ spacing if projecting).
- **R1003 Masonry chimney** — **3-2-10 rule**: top **≥ 3 ft** above roof penetration **and ≥ 2 ft above** anything within **10 ft**; wall ≥ **4 in** nominal masonry; flue area ≥ **1/10–1/12** of fireplace opening; combustible clearance **2 in** (interior) / **1 in** (exterior).
- **R1004/R1005 Factory-built** — listed/labeled (UL 127 fireplaces, Type HT chimney for solid fuel), installed per listing; ≤ 30° offsets. **R1006** exterior combustion-air supply.

# PART IV — Energy (Chapter 11) 🏠
**[RE] Energy Efficiency** = the **Michigan residential energy provisions** (IECC-based). MI is **Climate Zone 5** (most counties) / **6/7** (north & U.P.). Key prescriptive (Table N1102.1.2/R402.1.2, **CZ 5**): ceiling **R-49**, wood wall **R-20 or 13+5ci**, floor **R-30**, basement wall **R-15/19**, slab **R-10 @ 4 ft**, crawl wall **R-15/19**, window **U ≤ 0.30**, **air sealing / blower-door ≤ 3 ACH50**, duct testing. (CZ 6/7 increase wall to R-20+5 etc. — verify county zone.)

# PARTS V–VIII — Mechanical / Fuel Gas / Plumbing / Electrical (pointers)
- **Ch 12–23 Mechanical** 🏠 — appliance install, clearances, **whole-house & local ventilation (M1505)**, dryer exhaust (**≤ 35 ft**, M1502), combustion air (Ch 17), duct sizing, **dwelling-unit HVAC**. Mirrors the Michigan Mechanical Code.
- **Ch 24 Fuel Gas** 🏠 — gas piping sizing, appliance connectors, venting, CSST bonding (cf. MEC §250.104(B)). Mirrors the Michigan Fuel Gas / IFGC.
- **Ch 25–33 Plumbing** 🏠 — fixtures, DWV, water supply/distribution, water heaters; mirrors `Michigan-Plumbing-Code-2021-reference.md` (e.g., **P2708** showers ≥ 900 in², **P3201** traps 2–4 in seal, **P2903** supply pressure).
- **Ch 34–43 Electrical** 🏠 — dwelling wiring; mirrors `Michigan-Electrical-Code-2023-reference.md` (e.g., **E3901** receptacle spacing 6/12 ft, **E3902** GFCI/AFCI, **E3801** services). *Note:* the standalone Michigan Electrical Code (NEC 2023) is the enforced electrical code; the MRC's electrical chapters track the NEC but may lag a cycle — verify.
