# Michigan Plumbing Code 2021 — Quick Reference

> **Source:** [UpCodes — Michigan Plumbing Code 2021](https://up.codes/viewer/michigan/ipc-2021)
> (merged text: International Plumbing Code 2021, 2nd printing + Michigan Part 7 amendments).
> Companion file: `2021-Michigan-Plumbing-Code-Part7-Final-Rules-eff-2024-03-12.pdf` (the raw Michigan amendments, eff. 2024-03-12).
>
> ⚠️ **Scope caveat for this project:** Per **§101.2**, the *Michigan Plumbing Code* does **not** cover
> detached one- and two-family dwellings — those are governed by the **Michigan Residential Code**
> (which mirrors these provisions almost exactly). For a single-family home design, treat the numbers
> below as the controlling intent; verify any borderline call against the MRC. Items most relevant to a
> private home are flagged **🏠**.
>
> Units: dimensions cross-listed in inches/mm and °F/°C as in the code. `gpf` = gallons per flush,
> `gpm` = gallons per minute, `DFU` = drainage fixture unit, `WSFU` = water supply fixture unit.

## Chapter 1 — Scope & Administration
*(heavily Michigan-amended; admin only)*

- **101.1 Title** — Known as the *Michigan plumbing code*.
- **101.2 Scope** 🏠 — Governs installation/replacement/alteration/relocation/use of plumbing systems in buildings **except detached 1–2 family dwellings** (→ Michigan Residential Code).
- **101.3** — Stille-DeRossett-Hale Single State Construction Code Act (1972 PA 230) takes precedence.
- **104** — Code official enforces & interprets; may inspect with credentials/warrant.
- **105** — Alternative materials/methods allowed if equal to code intent.
- **106 Permits** — Most work needs a permit; minor repairs/fixture swaps exempt (106.2). Permit expires if work not begun within **180 days** or abandoned **180 days** (106.5.3).
- **108** — Required inspections: underground, rough-in, final. Test per §312.
- **115.4** — Violations → licensing action under Skilled Trades Regulation Act (2016 PA 407).

## Chapter 2 — Definitions (key terms)

- **Air gap (drainage):** unobstructed vertical distance through free air between a waste pipe outlet and the flood level rim.
- **Air break (drainage):** drain discharges *below* the flood level rim but *above* the trap seal.
- **Backflow:** reverse flow into the potable supply; **Backflow preventer:** assembly/device/method preventing it.
- **Branch:** any part of the piping system except a riser, main, or stack.
- **Branch interval:** vertical distance ≥ 8 ft of developed length between horizontal-branch connections to a drainage stack.
- **Building drain:** lowest piping of the drainage system; receives soil/waste pipes inside and extends **30 in** beyond the exterior wall.
- **Building sewer:** from the end of the building drain to the public sewer / disposal system.
- **Cleanout:** access opening for removing obstructions.
- **Developed length:** length measured along the centerline of pipe + fittings.
- **DFU (drainage fixture unit):** measure of probable discharge into the drainage system.
- **Flood level rim:** edge of a receptacle from which water overflows.
- **Horizontal branch drain:** lateral branch off a soil/waste stack receiving ≥ 2 fixture drains.
- **Stack:** any vertical soil/waste/vent/conductor line through ≥ 1 story.
- **Stack vent:** extension of a soil/waste stack above the highest horizontal drain connected to it.
- **Vent stack:** vertical vent installed mainly to circulate air to/from the drainage system.
- **Trap:** fitting providing a liquid seal to block sewer gases without materially affecting flow. **Trap seal:** vertical distance between weir and top of trap dip.
- **Water service pipe:** from the main/source to the building's water distribution system. **Water distribution pipe:** from the service pipe to points of use.
- **Bathroom group:** WC + lavatory + bathtub/shower on the same floor level.
- **Fixture branch:** a drain serving ≥ 2 fixtures discharging to another drain or stack.

## Chapter 3 — General Regulations

**Materials / certification**
- 303.3 — Plastic pipe/fittings third-party certified to **NSF 14**. 303.1 — every pipe & fitting marked with maker ID.

**304 Rodentproofing** — Drain strainer openings **≤ ½ in** least dimension; seal pipe penetrations.

**305 Protection of pipes** 🏠
- 305.4 — Protect from freezing; exterior water supply **≥ 6 in below frost line** and **≥ 12 in below grade**.
- 305.4.1 — Building sewer **≥ 42 in below grade** (private disposal line ≥ 8 in to top of pipe).
- 305.6 — Pipe < **1¼ in** from stud/joist edge → **No. 16 gage (0.0575 in)** steel shield plate, extending ≥ 2 in past plates.
- 305.3 — Pipe through foundation: sleeve **2 pipe sizes** larger.

**306 Trenching/backfill** — Continuous load-bearing support; backfill in **6-in** tamped layers; **12 in** cover over crown before normal backfill.

**307 Structural safety** 🏠 — Cutting/notching/boring per Michigan Building Code; no truss alteration without design professional (307.4).

**308 Piping support — Table 308.5 max hanger spacing** 🏠

| Material | Horizontal | Vertical |
|---|---|---|
| PVC (DWV) | 4 ft | 10 ft |
| ABS | 4 ft | 10 ft |
| Cast iron | 5 ft (10 ft if 10-ft lengths) | 15 ft |
| Copper tube ≤ 1¼ in | 6 ft | 10 ft |
| Copper tube ≥ 1½ in | 10 ft | 10 ft |
| Copper pipe | 12 ft | 10 ft |
| PEX ≤ 1 in | 32 in (2.67 ft) | 10 ft |
| PEX ≥ 1¼ in | 4 ft | 10 ft |
| CPVC ≤ 1 in | 3 ft | 10 ft |
| Steel pipe | 12 ft | 15 ft |

> Vertical DWV ≤ 2 in needs a mid-story guide. Hangers must prevent galvanic action (308.3).

**310 Toilet/washroom** — Illumination & ventilation per building/mechanical code; finish per building code.

**312 Tests** (rough-in) 🏠
- Water test: ≥ **10-ft head**, hold **≥ 15 min**, tight at all points (312.2).
- Air test (DWV): **5 psi** / 10-in mercury column, hold **≥ 15 min** (312.3).
- Water supply test: working pressure or **≥ 50 psi** air, hold **≥ 15 min** (312.5).
- Shower liner: dam, flood **≥ 2 in** at threshold, hold **≥ 15 min** (312.9).

**314 Condensate** 🏠 — Min **¾-in** drain, slope **⅛ in/ft (1%)**; discharge to approved indirect receptor; auxiliary/secondary pan or shutoff where overflow could cause damage. Pan ≥ **1½ in** deep, ≥ **3 in** larger than unit.

**315 Penetrations** — Seal annular spaces; fire-rated assemblies per MBC §714.

## Chapter 4 — Fixtures, Faucets & Fixture Fittings 🏠

**Clearances (405.3.1)** — the big three:
- Water closet centerline → wall/partition/fixture: **≥ 15 in**.
- Between two fixtures, centerline to centerline: **≥ 30 in**.
- Clear space in front of WC/bidet: **≥ 21 in**.
- WC compartment: width ≥ 30 in; depth ≥ 60 in (floor-mounted) / 56 in (wall-hung).

**Urinals (405.3.5)** — ≥ 30 in between partitions; partitions ≤ 12 in off floor, ≥ 60 in tall, project ≥ 18 in (commercial — rarely a home item).

**Shower compartment (421.4)** — interior ≥ **900 in²** and least dimension ≥ **30 in** (may drop to 25 in least dim if ≥ 1,300 in²), measured to ≥ 70 in height.

**Max flow rates (401.3 / 604.4)**
| Fixture | Max |
|---|---|
| Water closet | **1.6 gpf** |
| Showerhead | **2.5 gpm @ 80 psi** |
| Lavatory faucet (residential) | 2.2 gpm @ 60 psi |
| Kitchen faucet | 2.2 gpm @ 60 psi |
| Urinal | 0.5 gpf |

**Fixture count — Table 403.1, Residential R-3 (1–2 family)** 🏠
- Per dwelling unit: **1 water closet, 1 lavatory, 1 bathtub/shower, 1 kitchen sink, 1 clothes-washer connection.**

**424 Faucets/showers** — tub/shower valves must be balanced-pressure / thermostatic / combination; max outlet **120 °F** (scald protection).

## Chapter 5 — Water Heaters 🏠

- **502.5 Clearance** — level working space **≥ 30 in × 30 in** in front of the control/service side.
- **501.2 Combo space-heating** — mixing valve (ASSE 1017) to cap potable hot water at **≤ 140 °F**.
- **504.4–504.5 T&P relief valve** — required; ANSI Z21.22; temp setting **≤ 210 °F**, pressure ≤ tank rating or **150 psi**, whichever less. **No** shutoff/check between valve and tank.
- **504.4.1** — temperature element senses water in **top 6 in** of tank.
- **504.6 Discharge pipe** — rigid, rated ≥ 210 °F, **same dia. as valve outlet**, gravity drain, no valves/threads at end, terminate **≤ 4 in above floor** (or to a pan/waste receptor), no traps.
- **504.7 Pan** — galv. steel/aluminum ≥ 0.0236 in (or plastic ≥ 0.036 in, not under gas), depth **≥ 1½ in**, **¾-in** indirect drain to floor drain or outdoors (6–24 in above grade). *(Not required on a like-for-like replacement if none existed.)*

## Chapter 8 — Indirect & Special Waste 🏠

- **802.1.1** — food storage/prep/handling equipment discharges through an **indirect** waste with an **air gap**.
- **802.3.1 Air gap** — ≥ **twice** the effective opening of the indirect waste pipe (above flood rim).
- **802.3.2 Air break** — between indirect waste pipe and the receptor trap seal.
- **802.4.3 Standpipe (clothes washer)** 🏠 — height **18 in min – 42 in max** above the trap weir; min **2-in** pipe; trap 6–18 in above floor.
- Dishwasher 🏠 — discharge via air gap or high loop to disposer/sink tailpiece (no direct drain connection).

## Chapter 9 — Vents 🏠

- **906 Sizing** — vent ≥ **half the drain diameter**, never **< 1¼ in**. Vents > 40 ft developed length: bump one nominal size. Stack/vent stacks per **Table 906.1** (developed length × DFU).
- **903.1 Roof termination** — extend **≥ 12 in above roof** (≥ 7 ft if roof is occupied).
- **903.5 Openings** — vent terminal **≥ 10 ft horizontally** from any door/window/air intake, *or* **≥ 3 ft above** it.
- **903.1.4 Sidewall** — ≥ 10 ft from lot line and ≥ 10 ft above grade.

**Max trap-to-vent (trap arm) distance — Table 909.1** 🏠
| Trap/drain | Slope | Max distance |
|---|---|---|
| 1¼ in | ¼"/ft | 5 ft |
| 1½ in | ¼"/ft | 6 ft |
| 2 in | ¼"/ft | 8 ft |
| 3 in | ⅛"/ft | 12 ft |
| 4 in | ⅛"/ft | 16 ft |

**Venting methods**
- **910 Individual vent** — each trap its own vent.
- **911 Common vent** — one vent serves 2 traps at the same level.
- **912 Wet vent** 🏠 — vents a bathroom group(s) via the drain of higher fixtures; common in homes.
- **914 Circuit vent** — ≤ 8 fixtures on a horizontal branch (commercial).
- **915 Combination waste & vent** — sinks/lavs/floor drains/fountains; slope ½"/ft.
- **918 Air admittance valves (AAV)** 🏠 — ASSE 1051 (individual/branch) / 1050 (stack); install ≥ 4 in above drain and ≥ 6 in above flood rim; at least one stack vent must still reach open air.

## Chapter 10 — Traps, Interceptors & Separators 🏠

- **1002.1** — each fixture gets a **separate trap**; trap arm: fixture outlet → trap weir **≤ 24 in** vertical (horizontal offset measured per trap-arm rules).
- **1002.4 Trap seal** — **2 in min – 4 in max** (deep-seal where evaporation likely).
- **1002.2** — slip joints only at inlet, outlet, and seal; **1002.7** traps set level.
- **1003.3 Prohibited** 🏠 — **S-traps, bell traps, crown-vented traps, drum traps, traps with moving parts, double trapping.** (Use a P-trap + vent.)
- **Grease interceptors** — required for restaurants/cafeterias etc. (commercial); sized by peak gpm × 30-min retention (1003.3). Not a home item.

## Chapter 11 — Storm Drainage

- **Sizing** — based on **100-year, 1-hr rainfall** (Fig. 1106.1) × roof area; gpm = (rainfall in/hr × area ft²) ÷ 96 (§1106.2.1). Pipe capacity from **Table 1106.2** (e.g., 4-in @ ⅛"/ft ≈ 115 gpm).
- **Min slope** 1/16 in/ft (horizontal).
- **1108 Secondary (overflow) drains/scuppers** required where roof edge dams water; discharge separately, above grade.
- **1101.3** — storm water **must not** connect to a sanitary-only sewer; floor drains not on storm (1104.2).
- 🏠 For a house this is mostly **gutters & downspouts** (governed by the building/residential code) rather than interior conductors.

## Chapter 12 — Special Piping & Storage Systems
*(Not a residential item.)* — Nonflammable medical gas / nonmedical oxygen systems; comply with **NFPA 99 / 51 / 55** (§§1201–1203).

## Chapter 13 — Nonpotable Water Systems
- Covers on-site nonpotable reuse / graywater (1302), rainwater harvesting (1303), reclaimed water (1304).
- **Purple-pipe** marking + clear separation from potable; **backflow / cross-connection** protection required.
- 🏠 Relevant only if the home adds a graywater or rainwater-harvest system.

## Chapter 14 — Subsurface Graywater Soil Absorption
- Subsurface landscape-irrigation disposal of graywater: 1401 general, 1402 design/sizing (soil percolation), 1403 install. 🏠 Only if the home uses a subsurface graywater field.
